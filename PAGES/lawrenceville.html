Lawrenceville

Where History Meets Innovation

Welcome to Lawrenceville

Discover one of Pittsburgh's oldest and most vibrant neighborhoods, where historic charm meets contemporary creativity. Located less than three miles from Downtown Pittsburgh, Lawrenceville has transformed from its industrial roots into a thriving cultural hub.

About Lawrenceville

Founded in 1814 and named after Captain James Lawrence from the War of 1812, Lawrenceville boasts a rich history that spans over two centuries. In 2019, it was designated as a Historic District and added to the National Register of Historic Places, recognizing its architectural significance and cultural heritage. The neighborhood's transformation from industrial center to creative district exemplifies Pittsburgh's own evolution.

Notable Landmarks

Lawrenceville is home to several historic landmarks, including the Allegheny Arsenal, the iconic Doughboy Statue located at the intersection of Butler Street and Penn Avenue, the Carnegie Library - Lawrenceville Branch, and the Stephen Foster House. These sites tell the story of the neighborhood's military, cultural, and social history.

Unique Characteristics

With Butler Street and Penn Avenue serving as its main arteries, Lawrenceville has become a haven for artists and creatives. The neighborhood features a vibrant cultural scene with numerous art galleries and studios, a diverse architectural landscape, and a neighborhood center anchored by a bowling alley. The rich mix of restaurants, bars, and retail shops makes it a destination for locals and visitors alike.

Local Dining Spotlight: Driftwood Oven

Starting as a mobile wood-fired pizza trailer, Driftwood Oven has grown into a beloved Butler Street restaurant known for naturally leavened sourdough crusts and locally sourced ingredients. The eatery has gained national praise, including a James Beard nomination, for its artisanal pies, breads, and hearty sandwiches. It's a must-visit for anyone exploring Lawrenceville's vibrant food scene.
